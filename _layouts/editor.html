As an academic Pediatric Urologist my career goal is to provide excellent clinical care to children with Pediatric Urologic problems now and to ensure continually improving care, through evidence based research, for the future. I am committed to continuing my own funded research interests in prenatal hydronephrosis and bladder outlet obstruction, but I see my main role now is to support and mentor junior faculty so enabling them to produce consistently high quality research to further our field. With many previous fellows and faculty colleagues involved in excellent academic work both here and in Europe, I believe my biggest contribution now to the field of Pediatric Urology is to continue to help develop academic faculty. It is only through these endeavors that we can improve care for children.
